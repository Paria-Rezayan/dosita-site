Research Focus

  • Adaptive online system identification for real-time manoeuvring model updates.

  • Digital-twin benchmarking in MARUS using standard manoeuvres/metrics to validate generalisation.

Overview

Project Overview

DOSITA is a PhD research project focused on the design and development of an open-source, AI-enabled online system identification framework for autonomous marine vessels, particularly Unmanned Surface Vessels (USVs). The project centres on the real-time estimation of coupled hydrodynamic derivatives using data-driven methodologies, embedded within the high-fidelity simulation environment of MARUS (Marine Robotics Unity Simulator).

This research aims to contribute to the cost-effective decarbonisation and sustainable operation of autonomous marine vessels, aligned with the UK Net Zero target (Net Zero Strategy).

Motivation

Why DOSITA Matters

Maritime operations contribute approximately 3% of global greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions, placing growing pressure on maritime stakeholders to reduce their carbon footprint. Although sustainable fuels are under development, operational optimisation—particularly through state-of-the-art AI-enabled technologies—remains an immediate and effective pathway to reduce emissions [1] [2].

Research Direction

Research Questions

RQ1

How can a data-driven online identification framework enable real-time estimation of coupled hydrodynamic derivatives in nonlinear ship manoeuvring models?

RQ2

How can AI-enabled machine learning optimisation enhance the overall accuracy, noise robustness, and physical consistency of online nonlinear ship manoeuvring model identification?

RQ3

How can integration with a high-fidelity simulation and digital twin framework such as MARUS accelerate the development, validation, and deployment of intelligent and sustainable maritime systems?

RQ4

What is the balance between real-time simulation execution and the accuracy and reliability of hydrodynamic derivative estimation during validation and benchmarking?

Methodology

Pipeline

DOSITA connects MARUS-based virtual sea trials with structured data acquisition, online identification, AI optimisation, and benchmarking to produce reproducible manoeuvring model updates and validation metrics.

Benchmarking

Benchmarks & Validation

Standards-aligned manoeuvres

Turning Circle (±35° rudder-equivalent) and Zig-Zag (10/10 and 20/20) under steady approach conditions (SpeedHold + steady window) to produce system-identification-ready trajectories.

Core reported metrics

Turning Circle: Advance (Ad/L), Transfer (Tr/L), Tactical Diameter (Dt/L), steady diameter estimate, t90/t180, and max yaw rate. Zig-Zag: initial turning distance (to 10°)/L, 1st/2nd overshoot (deg), peak times, max yaw rate, and cross-track error (XTE/L).

Validation checks

IMO MSC.137-style criteria checks (pass/fail where applicable), trajectory deviation diagnostics, and consistency checks between simulated predictions and manoeuvring data.

Early baseline (illustrative)

Initial MARUS calibration and automated extraction outputs used as a reproducible benchmark baseline.

Turning Circle

  • Advance (Ad/L): ~2.30–2.37
  • Transfer (Tr/L): ~1.27–1.39
  • Tactical Diameter (Dt/L): ~3.44–3.61
  • Max yaw rate: ~6.2–6.4 deg/s

Zig-Zag (10/10 and 20/20)

  • Initial turning distance to 10°/L: ~0.49–0.86
  • 1st overshoot: ~0.26–0.45 deg
  • 2nd overshoot: ~0.29–0.55 deg
  • Max yaw rate: ~4.0–5.8 deg/s

Reporting + conventions

  • 3-DOF logging: x, y, ψ, u, v, r, δ̂cmd/δ̂exec proxies
  • Coordinate/heading alignment: simulator ↔ nautical conversion applied for metrics
  • Speed targeting: Vtarget derived from calibrated V85

Progress

Results & Current Milestones

Current milestones

  • Standards-aligned TC/ZZ automation and dataset logging.
  • Automated metric extraction pipeline for benchmark reporting.
  • Baseline manoeuvre benchmarks for port/starboard runs.

Next milestones

  • Online identification module integration (real-time derivatives).
  • Benchmark against literature hulls / published manoeuvre metrics.
  • Validation across manoeuvre families + robustness tests (noise, bias).

Planned outputs

  • Parametric state-space manoeuvring models (coupled derivatives).
  • Reproducible validation reports (metrics + criteria checks).
  • Open-source dataset + scripts for benchmarking.
