Project Overview

DOSITA is a PhD research project focused on the design and development of an open-source, AI-enabled online system identification framework for autonomous marine vessels, particularly Unmanned Surface Vessels (USVs). The project centres on real-time estimation of coupled hydrodynamic derivatives using data-driven methodologies, embedded within the high-fidelity simulation environment of MARUS (Marine Robotics Unity Simulator).

The goal is to deliver a reproducible pipeline that connects virtual sea trials to trustworthy model updates, enabling robust control and validation workflows that support sustainable maritime operation and emissions reduction.

Focus Online identification • Physical consistency • Benchmarking
Platform MARUS digital twin & simulation workflows
Outputs Open artefacts: code, datasets, manoeuvre metrics, PDFs

Why DOSITA Matters

Operational optimisation is an immediate pathway to reduce emissions in maritime systems. However, optimisation and control rely on manoeuvring models that are accurate, robust to noise, and consistent with physics. DOSITA aims to close this gap through an end-to-end online identification workflow with validation and benchmarking baked in.

Research Questions

RQ1

How can a data-driven online identification framework enable real-time estimation of coupled hydrodynamic derivatives in nonlinear ship manoeuvring models?

RQ2

How can AI-enabled machine learning optimisation enhance accuracy, noise robustness, and physical consistency in online nonlinear ship manoeuvring model identification?

RQ3

How can integration with a high-fidelity simulation and digital twin framework such as MARUS accelerate development, validation, and deployment of intelligent and sustainable maritime systems?

RQ4

What is the balance between real-time simulation execution and the accuracy and reliability of hydrodynamic derivative estimation during validation and benchmarking?

Pipeline

DOSITA connects MARUS-based virtual sea trials with structured data acquisition, online identification, ML-assisted optimisation, and benchmarking to produce reproducible manoeuvring model updates and validation metrics.
